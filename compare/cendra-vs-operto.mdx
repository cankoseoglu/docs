## Quick answer

Cendra and Operto overlap on AI guest messaging but come from different product roots. Operto is a smart-home-and-operations platform for hospitality — smart locks, noise monitoring, in-unit devices, guest service workflows — with Operto ONE Guest Services Agent as the AI messaging layer on top. Cendra is an AI-first operations platform for short-term rentals with three specialized agents (Guest, Sales, Operations) running at 70–80% autopilot.

Choose **Operto** if smart-home infrastructure (locks, sensors, thermostats, noise monitors) is core to your operation and you want the guest services AI integrated with that device layer. Choose **Cendra** if AI depth is your primary need — multi-agent automation across guest communication, upsells, and back-office operations — with smart-lock integration as a supporting feature, not the foundation.

## Where Cendra wins

### AI depth as the core bet

Cendra's whole product is AI automation — three specialized agents, per-property [knowledge bases](/ai-agents/knowledge-base), [AI rules](/ai-agents/ai-rules) and [escalations](/ai-agents/escalations), automatic [gap-surfacing](/ai-agents/knowledge-candidates), per-agent autopilot control.

Operto's product is smart-home infrastructure with an AI guest services module layered on top. Both are legitimate approaches; Cendra just invests deeper on the AI axis because that's our core bet.

### 70–80% autopilot target

Cendra targets 70–80% full autopilot message handling within 90 days. Operto ONE offers 24/7 availability, instant responses, and operator-claimed 70% automation of inquiries. Comparable targets — the difference is which product surface area gets the R&D. Cendra pours it into AI specialization. Operto pours it into device-integrated workflows.

### STR-native vs hotel-hybrid

Operto serves hotels and STR together, with strong device-infrastructure roots. Cendra is STR-only. If your operation is pure STR, that specialization matters for roadmap alignment.

### Home Automation — agentic device control, not rule-based

Cendra ships agentic Home Automation as a first-class capability, not a bolt-on. Event-driven rules execute automatically:

- **30 minutes before check-in** → pre-heat/cool the unit, generate one-time access code, send to guest
- **After check-out** → lock doors, revoke codes, set to "away" mode

The agentic part is what's different from traditional smart-home platforms. Cendra's Home Automation rules are exposed as tools to the AI agents, meaning the Guest Agent and Operations Agent can create, manage, and run automations on behalf of operators. Example: if a vendor needs 2am emergency access to fix a leak, the Operations Agent generates a time-boxed one-time access code, sends it to the vendor via SMS, and revokes it once the job is verified complete — without waking you.

Operto handles device orchestration with strong depth but isn't natively wired to an AI agent system that can author and run new automations on the fly. If you want AI agents that *compose* device workflows dynamically (not just trigger pre-configured ones), Cendra's agentic layer is the differentiator.

### Operations Agent works with or without device infrastructure

Cendra's [Operations Agent](/ai-agents/how-agents-work) opens tasks from guest messages autonomously and routes them, whether or not you have smart locks, thermostats, or noise monitors installed. If you do have devices, Home Automation extends what the agent can do (access codes, climate, lock state). If you don't, the agent still runs ops end-to-end.

### Outcome-based pricing

Cendra's price scales with automation rate. Operto's pricing combines device fees with software subscription. If automation (not device monitoring) is your primary need, outcome-based pricing aligns more directly with value.

### Operator-founded

Cendra's CEO ran a 60-apartment STR portfolio. Operto comes from a longer hospitality-tech history. Different founder lenses — worth weighing against your own operator reality.

## Where Operto wins

- **Smart-lock + device infrastructure matters**. If you run properties where keyless access, noise monitoring, thermostat control, and in-unit device orchestration are core to your operating model, Operto's device depth is a genuine advantage.
- **Hotel + STR portfolios**. Operto's hotel-hospitality heritage gives it stronger fit for operators running both.
- **Device-driven ops workflows**. Ops automations triggered by device events (lock unlocked, noise threshold crossed, thermostat adjusted) are an Operto strength.
- **Unified platform across devices + AI**. If you want one vendor handling your physical infrastructure plus AI messaging, Operto's consolidation is valuable.

## Feature-level deep dive

### Guest communication

Operto ONE: 24/7 AI guest messaging integrated with device events. Cendra: multi-agent guest communication with structured [knowledge base](/ai-agents/knowledge-base) and per-agent autopilot control.

### Operations orchestration

Operto: device-event-driven automations + guest service workflows. Cendra: autonomous [Operations Agent](/ai-agents/how-agents-work) that detects ops signals from messages and opens/routes tasks independently of device state.

Operto: core native product with deep lock integrations. Cendra: [smart lock support](/smart-locks/overview) plus agentic Home Automation rules — event-driven pre-heat/cool before check-in, automatic lock + code revocation after check-out, on-demand one-time vendor codes generated by AI agents.

### Upsells

Operto: varies by PMS + service configuration. Cendra's Sales Agent: pre-built upsell workflows (late-checkout, early-check-in, gap-night, custom offers) with configurable per-property pricing.

### Channels

Cendra consolidates into a [unified inbox](/inbox/unified-inbox) across WhatsApp, Gmail, Outlook, and all major OTAs. Operto connects to PMS messaging channels + smart-device alerts.

### Languages

Cendra: [90+ languages](/ai-agents/multi-language) with auto-detection per agent. Operto: multi-language.

## Ideal customer for each

**Pick Cendra if:**

- AI automation depth is your primary need
- You don't have or don't want deep smart-device infrastructure
- You're STR-focused (not hotel)
- Ops orchestration (maintenance, vendor dispatch) matters, and you want it autonomous from message signals
- You want outcome-based pricing aligned to automation rate

**Pick Operto if:**

- Smart locks, noise monitoring, thermostats, and in-unit devices are core to your operation
- You run hotels + STR hybrid portfolios
- Device-event-driven automations are a core workflow
- You want a single vendor for physical infrastructure + AI messaging

## FAQs

### Is Operto mainly for hotels?
Operto serves both hotels and STR. Its infrastructure + device depth originated in hotel operations and extends into STR.

### Does Cendra handle smart locks?
Yes — [smart lock support](/smart-locks/overview) with agent-generated access codes for guests and vendors. It's not our primary product focus but it works reliably for the common STR use cases.

### Can I use Cendra alongside Operto?
Technically yes, but there's overlap on AI guest messaging. Most operators pick one AI layer.

### Which has higher automation?
Both target ~70% automation. Operto's numbers are tied to device-integrated workflows. Cendra targets 70–80% full autopilot on message handling within 90 days.

### Does Cendra have thermostat control and access code automation?
Yes. Cendra's Home Automation rules handle event-driven climate control (pre-heat before check-in, set-to-away after check-out) and automatic access code generation/revocation. Noise monitoring isn't currently in our native stack — for operators who need that specifically, Operto's dedicated hardware is a better fit.

### How fast is setup?
Cendra: under 30 minutes via PMS marketplace. Operto: device installation + PMS integration typically longer.

### What's the cost difference?
Cendra: outcome-based, scales with automation rate. Operto: device fees + software subscription. Different pricing shapes for different operator profiles.
