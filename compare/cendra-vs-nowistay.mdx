## Quick answer

Cendra and Nowistay both target high AI automation for short-term rental operators, but the deployment model is fundamentally different. Nowistay is an AI-native PMS — built from the ground up around AI, including fully autonomous guest replies and an MCP server for Claude/ChatGPT — which means you migrate your entire PMS to Nowistay to use it. Cendra is a specialized AI operations platform that layers on top of the PMS you already have (45+ supported, including Guesty, Hostaway, Lodgify, Hospitable, and more) running three specialized agents at 70–80% autopilot.

Choose **Nowistay** if you're ready to switch your PMS and want AI-first architecture from the ground up. Choose **Cendra** if you want production-grade AI automation without migrating your PMS — keep what's working for your reservations, channels, and operations, and add Cendra for specialist AI.

## The core choice

Nowistay is asking you to replace your PMS. That's a meaningful decision — months of historical data, integration muscle memory, team training, and connected tools (channel managers, cleaning tools, owner portals) are tied to your current PMS. Replacing your PMS to get AI is a big swing.

Cendra is asking you to keep everything you have and add an AI layer. That's a much smaller commitment with comparable AI outcomes. If AI-native PMS architecture is genuinely superior for your workflow, Nowistay may be worth the migration. For most operators, the switching cost isn't worth the marginal AI-native advantage.

## Where Cendra wins

### Zero migration cost

Keep your existing PMS (Guesty, Hostaway, Lodgify, Hospitable, Cloudbeds, OwnerRez, or any of 40+ others) and add Cendra as the AI layer. Your reservations, channels, calendar sync, accounting, and team workflows stay exactly as they are. Most operators are running in under 30 minutes via PMS marketplace connection.

Nowistay requires you to migrate your PMS. Time cost, training cost, integration-rebuild cost — those are real and often large.

### Runs on whichever PMS is best for your workflow

Different operators prefer different PMS for different reasons — Guesty for enterprise scale, Hostaway for value, Hospitable for direct booking + smart locks, Lodgify for direct booking websites, Cloudbeds for hotel/STR hybrid. Cendra works on all of them. See [supported integrations](/overview/supported-integrations).

Nowistay forces a single PMS choice — theirs. If your existing PMS is already well-matched to your workflow, switching to get AI is a downgrade on the non-AI dimensions.

### Multi-agent architecture built for STR operations

Cendra's three specialized agents cover the full operator workflow:

- **Guest Agent** for 24/7 communication
- **Sales Agent** for upsell workflows
- **Operations Agent** for task creation, vendor dispatch, maintenance follow-through

See [how agents work](/ai-agents/how-agents-work). Nowistay's AI is integrated into the PMS but the agent-level specialization is different — a single AI-native platform vs separate tunable agents.

### Outcome-based pricing

Cendra's price scales with your actual automation rate. Nowistay uses per-property subscription starting around €9/property/month.

For small portfolios the Nowistay model is cheaper. For mid-market (30+ properties) and enterprise scale, outcome-based pricing generally comes out ahead — you pay as automation rises, not per listing regardless of automation.

### Operator-founded product decisions

Cendra's CEO ran a 60-apartment STR portfolio. Every product decision traces back to operator pain — and crucially, the decision to *not* build a PMS was itself an operator-founded decision (operators already have PMS they like; making them switch is a huge ask).

Nowistay is a newer AI-native platform with a different product philosophy. Worth evaluating their roadmap if the PMS-migration trade-off appeals.

## Where Nowistay wins

Honest assessment — Nowistay is a real competitor and the AI-native approach has genuine benefits:

- **AI-first architecture**. Starting from zero with AI at the core means there's no legacy PMS logic to retrofit. For operators who view their current PMS as a bottleneck, this is appealing.
- **Fully autonomous AI (no host review needed)**. Nowistay's public position is that fully autonomous AI is the future and they ship it today. Cendra supports autopilot too but with more granular operator control.
- **MCP server for Claude / ChatGPT**. Nowistay ships an MCP server so you can interact with your property data conversationally via Claude or ChatGPT. Cendra doesn't currently ship this (though the underlying architecture supports similar workflows).
- **Simpler single-vendor stack**. One platform instead of PMS + Cendra-on-top. Operationally cleaner if you're happy with Nowistay's PMS depth.
- **Aggressive per-property pricing**. Starting at €9/property/mo makes Nowistay attractive for small and price-sensitive operators.

## Feature-level deep dive

### Guest communication

Both target autonomous AI. Nowistay positions as fully autonomous (no host review). Cendra supports autopilot per-agent per-property — operators can run Guest Agent on autopilot while keeping Sales Agent in copilot for specific properties. See [AI rules](/ai-agents/ai-rules).

### Upsells

Both handle upsells. Cendra's Sales Agent has pre-built flows for late-checkout, early-check-in, gap-night, and custom offers with configurable per-property pricing. Nowistay integrates upsells into the AI-native PMS.

### Operations

Cendra's [Operations Agent](/ai-agents/how-agents-work) opens tasks autonomously from message signals, routes them, follows up. Nowistay handles ops inside the PMS with AI assist — different approach, comparable outcome for well-configured setups.

### Channel management

Nowistay is the channel manager natively. Cendra doesn't do channel management — your existing PMS continues doing that work.

### Claude / ChatGPT integration

Nowistay's MCP server lets you query your PMS data via Claude or ChatGPT conversationally. Cendra doesn't ship this natively today. If this is a must-have for your workflow, Nowistay has the edge on that specific dimension.

### Smart locks and Home Automation

Cendra ships agentic Home Automation as a tools layer exposed to the AI agents. Event-driven rules execute automatically:

- 30 minutes before check-in → pre-heat/cool the unit + generate one-time access code
- After check-out → lock doors + revoke codes + set to "away"
- On-demand for emergencies → one-time time-boxed vendor access codes generated by the Operations Agent, revoked once the job is verified complete

The rules are exposed as tools to Cendra's agents, so the AI can *author* device workflows dynamically in response to guest messages — not just trigger pre-configured sequences. Nowistay's AI-native architecture doesn't feature device orchestration as prominently in public materials. See [smart lock support](/smart-locks/overview).

## FAQs

### Do I have to switch PMS to use Cendra?
No. Cendra integrates with 45+ existing PMS. You keep what you have. [Connect your PMS](/getting-started/connect-pms) in under 30 minutes.

### Do I have to switch PMS to use Nowistay?
Yes. Nowistay is the PMS — you migrate to their platform.

### Which has better AI?
Both are strong. Nowistay invests in AI-native architecture from scratch. Cendra invests in specialized multi-agent AI that works on top of existing PMS. The automation target (70–80%) is similar. Which is "better" depends on whether you're willing to migrate.

### Does Cendra have an MCP server?
Not currently. Nowistay has that today. If it's a must-have for your workflow, it's a real Nowistay advantage.

### Which is cheaper?
For 1–10 properties, Nowistay's €9/property is very cheap. For 20+ properties with high automation, Cendra's outcome-based pricing is competitive or cheaper. Both beat hiring a human team.

### Can I try both?
Yes. Cendra has a 1-week free trial (1 property autopilot). Nowistay offers demos.

### What's the risk of switching to Nowistay?
Migration cost is real — data, integrations, team training. If Nowistay works as advertised, the payoff is AI-native architecture. If parts of your workflow aren't well-served by their PMS (channel mix, unusual integrations, billing workflows), you may end up mid-migration with regrets.

### What's the risk of staying with my PMS + Cendra?
You're adding a vendor. Not migrating; but two vendors instead of one. For most operators this trade-off is worth it — your PMS keeps working and you get specialist AI without betting your operation on migration.
