## Quick answer

Cendra and Guesty ReplyAI are complementary, not competitive. Guesty ReplyAI is the draft-assist AI bundled into Guesty's PMS — since launch, ~40% of Guesty accounts use it and high-usage accounts have it drafting up to 50% of outgoing messages (which still require human send). Cendra is a specialized AI operations platform installed from the Guesty marketplace that runs Guest, Sales, and Operations agents at 70–80% full autopilot.

If you're on Guesty and ReplyAI's draft-and-send workflow is enough, you're set. But if you want **real autopilot** (not drafts), an **Operations Agent** that handles ops end-to-end, **outcome-based pricing** aligned to actual automation, and **specialized multi-agent reasoning** — Cendra layers on top of Guesty without replacing it.

The mental model: Guesty runs your bookings, channels, and operations. ReplyAI drafts replies. Cendra runs autonomously on top of both.

## Where Cendra wins

### Real autopilot, not "draft + I still have to click send"

Per Guesty's own launch announcement, ReplyAI has been adopted by over 40% of accounts who use it to save writing ~20% of messages sent. High-usage accounts have it drafting 50%. Those are drafts — a human still sends.

For mid-market and enterprise operators with thousands of messages per week, that human-in-the-loop bottleneck limits the real-world automation gain. Cendra targets 70–80% full autopilot — messages handled end-to-end without operator involvement — within 90 days. See [AI rules](/ai-agents/ai-rules) for per-property autopilot configuration.

### Three specialized agents, not one draft-writer

Guesty ReplyAI is a single generative feature. Cendra runs:

- **Guest Agent** — 24/7 guest communication across WhatsApp, email, and OTA channels
- **Sales Agent** — pre-built upsell workflows for late checkout, early check-in, gap nights, custom offers
- **Operations Agent** — task creation, vendor routing, maintenance follow-through

See [how agents work](/ai-agents/how-agents-work). The multi-agent split covers workflow breadth that a single draft-writer can't.

### Operations Agent runs the ops loop

Guesty has tasks and automations. But when a guest messages "the dishwasher is leaking," ReplyAI drafts a reply — it doesn't open a maintenance task, route it to the right vendor, track the fix, and notify the guest when done. Cendra's Operations Agent does all of that autonomously. See [issues and task management](/dashboard/issues-task-management).

### Deeper knowledge base with automatic gap-surfacing

ReplyAI references listing data and PMS context. Cendra builds a structured [per-property knowledge base](/ai-agents/knowledge-base) with house rules, amenities, access codes, local recommendations, and vendor contacts. When the AI encounters a question it can't answer, it surfaces a [knowledge candidate](/ai-agents/knowledge-candidates) for you to answer once — then remembers forever.

Over 90 days of operation, your knowledge base depth is the reason Cendra's automation rate keeps climbing while draft-only tools plateau.

### 90+ languages vs 12+

Cendra supports [90+ languages](/ai-agents/multi-language) with auto-detection and per-agent configuration. Guesty ReplyAI explicitly lists 12+ including English, Spanish, French, German, Chinese, Italian, and Portuguese. For portfolios with international guest mix, the language gap matters.

### Outcome-based pricing

Cendra's pricing scales with your actual automation rate. ReplyAI is included in Guesty subscription, so there's no direct cost to ReplyAI — but the cost of *still handling 80% of your messages manually* is the unmeasured bill.

## Where Guesty ReplyAI wins

- **No additional vendor relationship**. Included in your Guesty subscription. If ReplyAI's draft-assist is sufficient for your workflow, you have zero additional setup or spend.
- **Native PMS context**. Built directly on Guesty's data layer. No integration risk, no sync lag.
- **Low message volume workflows**. For small portfolios where draft-approve is fast enough, ReplyAI's simpler model works fine.
- **Conservative AI adoption curve**. Some teams prefer "AI suggests, human sends" as a permanent posture, not a ramp step. ReplyAI fits that philosophy.
